Black Fashion Week: Fear of God

THE SEVENTH COLLECTION - Spring 2021 Menswear

The Brand

Fear of God is a streetwear brand founded in 2013 by Jerry Lorenzo. The brand’s aesthetic is influenced by Lorenzo’s childhood memories playing baseball and 90s pop culture symbols like Kurt Cobain. Lorenzo has collaborated with Nike, PacSun, Ermenegildo Zenga, and Kanye West.

The Collection

The Seventh Collection is named after the number seven which symbolizes completeness and perfection. This collection is a statement of the brand’s “coming of age.” In this collection the brand debuts new offerings in the form of tailored garments, accessories, and knitwear. One of the most standout features of the collection are tributes to the Negro Leagues, which was inspired by Lorenzo’s family history and, according to the brand is, "a humble reminder of how far we’ve come and the responsibility to honor those before us who built the bridge we now cross."

Stylistically, the Seventh Collection is a departure from Fear of God’s streetwear origins. Gone are trendy, but short-lived streetwear pieces like the Nike Air Fear of God 1, which was featured in the brand’s Sixth Collection lookbook. The collection is very sophisticated and designed for function and longevity. While the pieces are more classic, they still present a streetwear edge that distinguishes them from offerings you would find at traditional luxury retailers. Overall, Fear of God is presenting a new vision of American luxury that unifies classic, luxury pieces with raw, culturally relevant streetwear aesthetics.
