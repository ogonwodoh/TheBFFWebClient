How Telfar has used Memes to Dominate 2020

Telfar’s multi-colored, functional Shopping Bag is probably the most popular bag of 2020. The bags are usually sold out and when they restock they sell out in minutes. They've been the topic of many news articles and have been worn by everyone from celebrities like Solange to politician Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez to the everyday person on social media.

Telfar bags have been so popular that the brand created the Bag Security Program so that everyone who wanted a Telfar bag could secure one.



What is Telfar?

Telfar is a luxury, unisex line established in 2005 in New York City. They sell clothing and accessories, but their most popular item is their Shopping Bag also known as the “Bushwick Birkin.” Telfar has been making the bag since 2014, but it has recently become super popular. The brand was created by Telfar Clemens. Clemens is a Liberian-American designer. Clemens' cares about social justice issues. In 2017 Clemens created a collection in collaboration with White Castle and the proceeds of the collection went to a bail fund for minors on Rikers Island. This summer the brand expressed support for the protests and riots over the killing of George Floyd. Overall, Clemens' brand embodies it's slogan, "Not for you, for everyone."


The Bag Frenzy

The frenzy over the bag is not just because it's aesthetically pleasing and relatively affordable, but is also because of Telfar's ingenious marketing through the usage of memes.

Memes are a highly effective way to disseminate information and Telfar has used this to their advantage. The memes that Telfar creates and reposts are a fun way of marketing the bags and make the brand more relatable and accessible. They create a positive feedback loop where people make funny Telfar memes, which the brand reposts, which encourages people to create more memes that help people discover the brand, and so on. The usage of this means of communication demonstrates that Telfar is a brand of the people and not just for elites. They are in touch with what the everyday person is feeling which adds to the appeal because the brand comes off as relatable.
