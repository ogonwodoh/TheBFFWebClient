16 Favorite Black Fashion Moments from Beyoncé's Black Is King

Black Is King, a retelling of the popular movie, The Lion King, was released at the end of July and has been a topic of conversation. Although the film has some shortcomings, it is undoubtably iconic due to it's rich visuals that were used to retell the story of the Lion King in a Pan-African lens. Beyoncé's stylist, Zerina Akers, curated the iconic imagery of Black is King that celebrated the beauty of being Black. Here we revist the most iconic Black fashion moments from the film.

  1. Beyoncé and her dancers wearing d.bleu.dazzled
    Beyoncé and her backup dancers stunned in d.bleu.dazzled outfits as they danced to the song “FIND YOUR WAY BACK” from The Lion King: The Gift album. d.bleu.dazzled specializes in crystalized lingerie, hoisery, and basics. The brand has also worked with Beyoncé on her Formation Tour and On The Run 2 Tour.

  2. Beyoncé's LACE BY TANAYA cape
    In scene for the song “FIND YOUR WAY BACK”, Beyoncé was also spotted in a crystal cape which was custom designed by LACE BY TANAYA. LACE BY TANAYA specializes in crystalized body jewelry.

  3. A Loza Maléombho custom-made outfit
    In the scene for the song “ALREADY” Beyoncé wore a custom Loza Maléombho jacket and bodysuit. Her hair was styled in bantu knots which tied the look together.

  4. L'Enchanteur accessories to match the Loza Maléombho outfit
    Beyoncé's Loza Maléombho outfit was paired with accessories from L'Enchanteur that perfectly matched.

  5. A Nigerian lace outfit by 5:31 JÉRÔME
    Jerome Lamaar, the designer behind 5:31 JÉRÔME, made a custom headpiece and trench/jumper hybrid for Beyoncé using Nigerian lace. This amazing usage of Nigerian lace appeared in the scene for the song “ALREADY”.

  6. A tribute to Nigerian 70's hairstyles and a Tongoro dress
    Tongoro studio is a Senegal based brand founded by Sarah Diouf. In the “BROWN SKIN GIRL” scene Beyoncé wore their NGOR dress. Her hair was in a style worn by Nigerian women in the 70s for special events.

  7. Beyoncé looking like a bag of money in Duckie Confetti
    Duckie Confetti designed this robe for Beyoncé that is now available for purchase on Beyoncé's website. The robe was worn in the scene for “MOOD 4 EVA”.

  8. Channeling Oshun in this yellow dress by Adama Paris
    Adama Amanda Ndiaye is the designer behind Adama Paris, which is based out of Dakar, Senegal. She designed this custom yellow dress that was seen in “BROWN SKIN GIRL”.

  9. Bringing fashion to chess with Melissa Simon-Hartman accessories
    Melissa Simon-Hartman designed the black chess piece characters in the “MOOD 4 EVA” scene.

  10. Custom tulle dresses by KEÄMA LLC
    Keama Garrett, the owner of KEÄMA LLC, designed these custom tulle dresses seen during the “WATER” song scene.

  11. Serving angles in a LEVENITY jacket
    Venny Etienne is an Haitian-American designer who is behind the brand LEVENITY. He designed this jacket for Beyoncé that she wore in the scence for the song “ALREADY”.

  12. Floral bodysuit by Garvey Collections
    Garvey Collections is a Brooklyn based brand by Sew.Me.Haute. Beyoncé wore the brand's floral bodysuit in the scene for “ALREADY”.

  13. Stunning jewelry by Beads Byaree
    Beads Byaree creates "Whimsical and Imaginative Wearable Art". They designed a choker worn by Blue Ivy in “BROWN SKIN GIRL”and earrings worn by one of the models in “MOOD 4 EVA”.

  14. DÉVIANT LA VIE and Alani Taylor experimenting with rope and lace
    DÉVIANT LA VIE created rich red rope outfits seen in “BIGGER”. Alani Taylor and DÉVIANT LA VIE collaborated to make the white lace and rope outfit spotted in “NILE”.

  15. Selam Fessahaye's first collection on Beyoncé
    Selam Fessahaye is a Swedish-Eritrean designer who created the beautiful dress worn by Beyoncé in the scene for “WATER”.

  16. Invoking African spirtuality in lafalaise-dion
    lafalaise-dion created several accessories worn by Beyoncé and the other models throughout the film in songs like “WATER” and “BROWN SKIN GIRL”.
